PRIVACY POLICY

FindMe! Privacy Policy

This Privacy Policy explains how FindMe! collects, uses, and protects your information when you use the app.

Effective date: 08 February 2026

1. Who we are

FindMe! is a mobile application that helps users discover nearby people and view the social media profiles and information they choose to share. This Privacy Policy applies only to the FindMe! app.

2. What data we collect

FindMe! is designed to collect only the minimum amount of information needed to provide its core functionality.

2.1. Account data

  • Email address (used for login, account security, and password reset)
  • Username
  • Authentication data provided by Firebase Authentication

2.2. Profile data

You may optionally provide the following profile information:

  • Profile photo
  • Profile rows (apps, services, or other information you choose to display)
  • Comment or bio text
  • Social media usernames or links (e.g., Instagram, Snapchat)

All profile data is provided voluntarily by you and is displayed to other users as part of the app’s discovery features. FindMe! does not access or interact with your social media accounts.

2.3. Location data

To show nearby users, FindMe! uses your device’s location with your permission.

  • Approximate GPS coordinates
  • Derived location representations (such as GeoHash)
  • Activity timestamps (e.g., lastUpdated, lastAppclose)

Location data is used solely to determine proximity and ensure that inactive or outdated users are not displayed. If location services are disabled, denied, or unavailable, nearby discovery features may be limited or unavailable.

2.4. Usage & device data

  • Push notification token (used only to deliver notifications)
  • Basic technical data (platform, language, app version)
  • Crash or error logs from service providers

3. How we use your data

  • Create and manage your account
  • Display your profile to other users
  • Show nearby users based on location and activity
  • Send push notifications when relevant
  • Maintain security, performance, and reliability
  • Prevent abuse or misuse of the service

You can control your visibility at any time. When you set your status to inactive, your profile and location data are not used for nearby discovery, and other users cannot see you.

4. Legal basis (EU/EEA)

  • Contract necessity – to provide core features
  • Legitimate interests – security and abuse prevention
  • Consent – for location services and notifications

5. How we share your data

We do not sell your personal data.

  • Service providers (Firebase / Google Cloud) for hosting, authentication, data storage, and notifications
  • Other users – limited to the profile information you choose to make visible, such as username, profile photo, profile rows, comment text, and social media handles

6. Data storage & retention

Account and profile data is stored as long as your account remains active. Location and presence data is retained only for a limited time to ensure accuracy.

7. Your choices & controls

7.1. Location permissions

You can manage location permissions in your device settings. If location access is disabled, nearby discovery features may not function.

7.2. Visibility & active status

You can switch between active and inactive status in the app settings. When inactive, your profile is hidden and not shown to other users.

7.3. Notifications

You can enable or disable push notifications at any time from your device settings.

7.4. Profile content

You can edit or remove your profile information at any time from within the app.

7.5. Stopping use of the app

You may stop using FindMe! at any time by logging out, uninstalling the app, or revoking permissions from your device.

8. Children’s privacy

FindMe! is not intended for children under the minimum age required by law. We do not knowingly collect personal data from children.

9. Changes to this policy

We may update this Privacy Policy from time to time. Changes will be reflected by updating the effective date.

10. Technical limitations & availability

FindMe! relies on internet connectivity, device permissions, and system services. If required permissions are disabled or services are unavailable, some features may not function. We do not process location or activity data when such services are unavailable.
